Project Details
Third Xinjiang Highway Project
Country:China, People's Republic of
Sector:Transport
Summary:

The highway project has both loan- and grant-financed activities:

A. Loan-financed components
  1. Capacity expansion of Kuitun–Sailimuhu Highway
    1. Construction of Kuitun–Sailimuhu Highway in Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region, consisting of (a) construction of about 303 kilometers (km) of highway between Kuitun and Sailimu Lake, including the construction of interchanges, service areas, and facilities for highway operation and maintenance; and (b) supply and installation of electrical and mechanical equipment for toll collection, traffic monitoring, lighting, and emergency telecommunications facilities; and
    2. Resettlement and rehabilitation of affected persons in connection with the highway expansion.
  2. Local roads rehabilitation program
    1. Rehabilitation of 12 sections of rural roads totaling about 600 km in length.
  3. Institutional strengthening and capacity building
    1. Strengthening the capacity of sector agencies and institutions in the areas of highway planning, design, construction supervision, operation and maintenance, and project management through the provision of training and equipment;
    2. carrying out of studies, providing technical advisory services, and providing specialized equipment in respect of road management improvement, road safety, toll highway management, and a transport services information system; and
    3. provision of equipment for the operation and maintenance of the arterial road network of the autonomous region.

B. First grant-financed activities
  1. Rural roads improvement in poor areas
    1. Development of appropriate criteria for inclusion of road segments under the project component, and of procedures to formulate annual work and investment programs under this project component;
    2. development of monitoring indicators; and
    3. identification of institutional strengthening needs.
  2. Ethnic minorities participation policy
    1. Conducting a social assessment to develop ethnic minorities’ development plans, in relation to the program of civil works and investment for the first year of project implementation.
  3. Traffic safety
    1. Review and evaluation of safety programs under other highway projects, traffic accident data, enforcement of traffic laws and regulations, vehicle inspection and driver training systems, and traffic safety education programs; and
    2. identification of institutional deficiencies for dealing with traffic safety and developing a program to address the needs for institutional strengthening.
  4. Preliminary engineering design review
    1. Review of the geometric–geotechnic–structural design of the highway; and
    2. review of the design of electrical and mechanical systems.
C. Second grant-financed activities
  1. Audit requirements
    1. Carrying out of technical audit of the final engineering design required for the major highway in the project and road safety audit; and
    2. provision of training for local staff of Xinjiang Communications Department in quality audit of technical designs.
Expected Outcome:The project will support economic and social development in Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region by improving its highway transport infrastructure and strengthening the institutional capacity of its highway sector.
Expected Outputs:A. Loan-financed components
  1. Kuitun–Sailimuhu Highway constructed and expanded, and affected persons resettled;
  2. 12 sections of rural roads with a total of 600 km rehabilitated; and
  3. institutional capacity of highway sector agencies and institutions strengthened, and operations and maintenance equipment installed.

B. First grant-financed activities
  1. Rural roads in poor areas improved and institutional capacity in these areas identified;
  2. ethnic minorities’ development plans implemented;
  3. traffic safety improved and institutional capacity strengthened; and
  4. geometric–geotechnic–structural design of the highway as well as electrical and mechanical systems design implemented.

C. Second grant-financed activities
  1. Engineering design and road safety audited; and
  2. training for local staff of Xinjiang Communications Department in quality audit of technical designs delivered.
Date Approved:5 September 2002
Status:Completed
Expected Completion:31 December 2009
